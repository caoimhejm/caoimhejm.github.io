Transistors

What are Transistors?

Transistors are like tiny electronic switches that control the flow of electricity in electronic devices. They can either let electricity flow or stop it, depending on what we want them to do. Think of them as the traffic lights of the electronic world, allowing or blocking the flow of electrical signals. Initially, transistors were small cylindrical objects, slightly larger than a pencil eraser. Over time, researchers and engineers have been able to make transistors tinier and tinier. With the invention of the integrated circuit, or microchip, in which thousands or millions of transistors are deposited on a piece of silicon, transistors have become microscopic.

Types of transistors

There are mainly two types of transistors, based on how they are used in a circuit.

1. Bipolar Junction Transistor (BJT)

The bipolar junction transistor (BJT) has three connection points known as terminals: the base, the emitter, and the collector. A very small current flowing between the base and emitter can control a larger flow of current between the collector and emitter terminal.

Furthermore, there are two types of BJT, and they are:

  • PNP Transistor: A PNP transistor has one n-type material between two p-type materials. It uses two diodes – one on the right (collector-base) and one on the left (emitter-base) – to control current flow.

  • NPN Transistor: An NPN transistor has one p-type material between two n-type materials. It's used to boost weak signals into strong ones. In an NPN transistor, electrons flow from the emitter to the collector, creating current. This type of transistor is commonly used in circuits.

2. Field Effect Transistor (FET)

In a FET, there are three parts: Gate, Source, and Drain. By adjusting the voltage at the gate, we can control the flow of current between the source and the drain. FETs are unipolar transistors(one-way switches for electrical signals), and they come in N-channel and P-channel types(like two types of electronic switches. N-channel turns on with a positive signal, allowing electricity to flow. P-channel turns on with a negative signal, and it stops the flow of electricity when it's on). They are often used in low-noise amplifiers, buffer amplifiers, and analog switches.

Other Types

There are many other types of transistors which include MOSFET, JFET, insulated-gate bipolar transistor, thin-film transistor, high electron mobility transistor and more.

Why are transistors important?

  • They drive computer memory chips and memory storage devices for MP3 players, smartphones, cameras and electronic games.
  • Transistors are deeply embedded in nearly all Integrated Circuits, which are part of every electronic device.
  • They can handle the heavy-duty work of converting power in your home and making sure your radio signals stay on the right track.
